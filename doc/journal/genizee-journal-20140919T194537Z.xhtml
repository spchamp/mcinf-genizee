Though the concept occurs in this same week as the previous, but it would be "Even more of a stretch," then — and in this following matter, "Completely off-topic" — to illustrate a relation, here, towards a study of the local proxy/remote proxy design of the DXPC protocol [LBX Mini-HOWTO]. The local proxy/remote proxy design of service architectures may be secondly implemented in a CORBA framework — in which, only each of the local proxy and the remote proxy (respectively ORBs) would need have access to the network (using IIOP) and local services would connect to the respective ORBs via something other than TCP/IP networking sockets.
